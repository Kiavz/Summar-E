Sense and Sensibility

"Sense and Sensibility" is Jane Austen's first published work, which was published in 1811. It is about two sisters and how their relationships affect them and their lives.

Summary

The Dashwood sisters, Elinor and Marianne, find themselves in a difficult situation after their father dies. Since their family estate was inherited by John Dashwood, their half-brother, the sisters, and their mother were left with lower incomes.

The older sister, Elinor Dashwood, embodies "sense", as she has a practical and reserved nature. She develops a romantic relationship with Edward Ferrars, though their relationship faces obstacles and Edward engages with another woman.

Marianne Dashwood on the other hand, represents "sensibility" because of her passionate and emotional disposition. She becomes fascinated with John Willoughby, but their relationship ends in heartbreak and disappointment.

>This novel explores the themes of love, expectations, sense, and sensibility. In the end, both Elinor and Marianne find love and happiness, but their journeys are far different from each other's.
